Brief Background of Myself

Posted on 2019.06.07 11:20 am

I came to the U.S. ten years ago for college. I studied at University of Washington and graduated with a bachelar of science degree in Construction Management. When I was in college, I interned with several companies including subcontractors and vendors. Now I am working in one of the largest general contractors companies in Washington State and have accumulated over five years of experiences in construction industry.

After I graduated, I often attended professional networking events and started making connections with people from different backgrounds and industries. Thus, I made so many friends from the events, and among them a lot work in technology industry. I was exposed to this field and every once in a while I got to hear their stories about their work and their lives. This later became one of the reasons why I want to become a programmer.


Why I Want to Become A Programmer

Posted on 2019.06.07 3:03 pm

Working in construction industry for the past several years has been a very rewarding experience to me. I have participated in a project at a jobsite to see it being built from a piece of land to a brand new high school that can teach 2,000 students. I also analyzed drawings and work scopes to generate construction estimates for office and residential towers ranging from $20M to over $100M dollars.

However, as technology progresses rapidly these days, there are more and more demands for people with technical or programming skills in most of the industries, even in construction, because technology is where the future lies. This makes me believe that having programming skills is critical to advance my career and to have more career opportunities. Additionally, with a lot of friends in technology field enjoying their work and lives, I became interested and started taking online tutorials and teaching myself how to do programming.


My Approach and My Goal

Posted on 2019.06.07 4:18 pm

After spending a couple of weeks learning programming online by myself, I decided to go with a more formal training to make sure I learn the basics and have a strong foundation of knowledge before I go too further. The reason that I choose to learn at Code Fellows is that it is widely acknowledged in the industry, and the certificate serves a strong proof that the person posesses competent skills and knowledge in programming. Moreover, I prefer to learn in a in-person classroom style as I find it more efficient for me to learn and get my questions answered right away.
